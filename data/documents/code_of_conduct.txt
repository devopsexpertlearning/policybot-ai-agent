================================================================================
OUR COMMITMENT
================================================================================

TechCorp is committed to maintaining the highest standards of business ethics
and professional conduct. This Code of Conduct outlines the principles and
standards that guide our actions and decisions.

All employees, contractors, and representatives of TechCorp are expected to
read, understand, and comply with this Code of Conduct.

================================================================================
1. CORE PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1.1 Integrity
We conduct business honestly and ethically in all circumstances. We keep our
commitments and admit our mistakes.

1.2 Respect
We treat everyone with dignity and respect, regardless of position, background,
or personal characteristics.

1.3 Responsibility
We take ownership of our actions and their consequences. We do what we say we
will do.

1.4 Excellence
We strive for the highest quality in our work and continuously seek to improve.

1.5 Fairness
We make decisions based on merit and facts, not favoritism or bias.

================================================================================
2. WORKPLACE CONDUCT
================================================================================

2.1 Professional Behavior
All employees must:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and courtesy
- Communicate professionally in all interactions
- Collaborate effectively with team members
- Maintain a positive and productive work environment
- Dress appropriately for the workplace and client meetings

2.2 Harassment and Discrimination
TechCorp has zero tolerance for harassment or discrimination.

Prohibited Conduct:
- Sexual harassment of any kind
- Discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age,
  disability, genetic information, sexual orientation, gender identity, or
  any other protected characteristic
- Bullying, intimidation, or threatening behavior
- Creating a hostile work environment
- Retaliation against those who report violations

Employees who experience or witness harassment must report it immediately.

2.3 Substance Abuse
- Illegal drug use is strictly prohibited
- Being under the influence at work is cause for disciplinary action
- Alcohol may be consumed only at approved company events
- Employees must be fit for duty at all times

2.4 Violence and Threats
- Threats of violence will not be tolerated
- Possession of weapons on company property is prohibited
- Report any concerns about workplace violence immediately

2.5 Workplace Romance
- Romantic relationships between employees must be disclosed if they create
  actual or perceived conflicts of interest
- Manager-subordinate relationships require disclosure and may require
  organizational changes
- Both parties must complete disclosure form

================================================================================
3. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
================================================================================

3.1 Definition
A conflict of interest arises when personal interests interfere, or appear to
interfere, with company interests.

3.2 Common Conflicts
Examples of potential conflicts:
- Outside employment with competitors or clients
- Financial interests in competing or partner companies
- Business relationships with family members
- Accepting gifts or entertainment from vendors
- Using company resources for personal business
- Hiring or supervising family members

3.3 Disclosure Requirements
Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their
manager and HR. Failure to disclose may result in disciplinary action.

3.4 Outside Employment
- Outside employment must not interfere with job performance
- Must not compete with TechCorp's business
- Must not use TechCorp resources, information, or time
- Director-level and above require approval for outside employment

================================================================================
4. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION
================================================================================

4.1 Confidential Information
Employees must protect confidential company information, including:
- Trade secrets and proprietary information
- Business strategies and plans
- Financial data
- Customer and partner information
- Employee personal information
- Product and technology development

4.2 Obligations
- Use confidential information only for business purposes
- Do not disclose to unauthorized parties
- Protect information with appropriate security measures
- Obligations continue after employment ends
- Return all confidential materials upon termination

4.3 Customer and Partner Data
- Handle customer data with strictest confidentiality
- Comply with all privacy laws and regulations
- Use data only for authorized purposes
- Never sell or share customer data inappropriately
- Report any data breaches immediately

================================================================================
5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
================================================================================

5.1 Company Ownership
All work product created within the scope of employment belongs to TechCorp:
- Software code and documentation
- Inventions and innovations
- Designs and creative works
- Business processes and methods
- Research and development

5.2 Respecting Others' IP
- Do not use others' intellectual property without proper licensing
- Respect copyrights, patents, and trademarks
- Use only properly licensed software
- Never incorporate third-party code without approval

5.3 Open Source
- Follow company policies for using open source software
- Obtain approval before using or contributing to open source projects
- Comply with all license requirements

================================================================================
6. BUSINESS ETHICS AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

6.1 Legal Compliance
Employees must comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including:
- Anti-corruption laws (Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, UK Bribery Act)
- Antitrust and competition laws
- Securities laws
- Export control laws
- Data protection and privacy laws
- Employment laws

6.2 Anti-Bribery and Corruption
TechCorp prohibits all forms of bribery and corruption:
- Never offer or accept bribes, kickbacks, or improper payments
- Do not make facilitation payments
- Exercise caution with gifts and entertainment
- Maintain accurate books and records
- Report any suspected corruption

6.3 Gifts and Entertainment

Giving Gifts:
- Must be legal, modest in value (<$100), and not create obligation
- Never give cash or cash equivalents
- Follow recipient organization's policies
- Obtain approval for gifts over $100

Receiving Gifts:
- May accept modest gifts in normal business context
- Decline anything that could create obligation or appearance of impropriety
- Never accept cash or cash equivalents
- Gifts over $100 must be disclosed to manager

Entertainment:
- Business meals and events are acceptable if reasonable and infrequent
- Attendance must have clear business purpose
- Avoid lavish or excessive entertainment
- Never attend events that could embarrass the company

6.4 Political Activities
- Political activities must be on personal time
- Do not use company resources for political purposes
- Do not imply company endorsement of candidates or positions
- Company may engage in lobbying in compliance with laws

6.5 Charitable Contributions
- Company supports charitable giving through approved programs
- Personal charitable work is encouraged on personal time
- Do not pressure others to support particular causes
- Do not use company resources without approval

================================================================================
7. FINANCIAL INTEGRITY
================================================================================

7.1 Accurate Records
All business records must be:
- Accurate and complete
- Created in timely manner
- Properly maintained and retained
- Available for authorized review

7.2 Expense Reporting
- Submit only accurate expense reports
- Include required receipts and documentation
- Obtain proper approvals
- Reimburse company for personal expenses charged in error

7.3 Time Reporting
- Accurately report all time worked
- Record PTO and leave correctly
- Never falsify time records
- Managers must review and approve timesheets promptly

7.4 Financial Reporting
- Maintain accurate financial books and records
- Never create false or misleading entries
- Comply with accounting standards and controls
- Cooperate with internal and external audits

================================================================================
8. COMPANY RESOURCES
================================================================================

8.1 Proper Use
Company resources should be used primarily for business purposes:
- Computer systems and software
- Office equipment and supplies
- Company vehicles
- Facilities and property
- Intellectual property

Limited personal use is acceptable if it:
- Does not interfere with work
- Does not incur significant cost
- Does not violate policies
- Is not for personal business gain

8.2 Protection of Assets
- Take reasonable steps to protect company property
- Report loss, theft, or damage
- Do not waste or misuse resources
- Secure company information and equipment

8.3 Social Media and Communications
- Do not disclose confidential information
- Be respectful and professional
- Clarify personal opinions are your own
- Do not claim to represent company without authorization
- Follow IT and social media policies

================================================================================
9. HEALTH, SAFETY, AND ENVIRONMENT
================================================================================

9.1 Workplace Safety
- Follow all safety rules and procedures
- Report hazards and unsafe conditions
- Use required safety equipment
- Participate in safety training
- Never work under influence of drugs or alcohol

9.2 Environmental Responsibility
- Comply with environmental laws and regulations
- Conserve resources and minimize waste
- Recycle where possible
- Report environmental concerns

================================================================================
10. REPORTING AND NON-RETALIATION
================================================================================

10.1 Duty to Report
Employees must promptly report:
- Violations of this Code of Conduct
- Illegal or unethical behavior
- Conflicts of interest
- Safety or security concerns
- Harassment or discrimination
- Financial irregularities

10.2 Reporting Channels
Report concerns through any of these channels:
- Direct manager
- Human Resources (hr@techcorp.com, ext. 8500)
- Legal Department (legal@techcorp.com)
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-0123 (anonymous)
- Online ethics portal: ethics.techcorp.com

10.3 Non-Retaliation Policy
TechCorp prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports concerns in good faith
- Participates in investigations
- Asks questions about ethical issues
- Refuses to violate the law or this Code

Retaliation is grounds for immediate termination.

10.4 Investigation Process
- All reports will be investigated promptly and thoroughly
- Confidentiality will be maintained to extent possible
- Employees must cooperate with investigations
- Appropriate action will be taken based on findings

================================================================================
11. CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATIONS
================================================================================

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory training
- Suspension
- Demotion
- Termination of employment
- Legal action
- Referral to law enforcement

The specific consequence depends on the nature and severity of the violation.

================================================================================
12. ACKNOWLEDGMENT AND QUESTIONS
================================================================================

All employees must:
- Read and understand this Code of Conduct
- Complete annual ethics training
- Sign acknowledgment form annually
- Ask questions when unsure about proper conduct

Questions about this Code should be directed to:
- Your manager
- Human Resources
- Legal Department
- Ethics Hotline

When in doubt, ask. It's always better to ask than to act improperly.

================================================================================

This Code of Conduct represents ourcommitment to doing business the right way.
Each of us plays a vital role in upholding these standards.

Together, we build a company we can be proud of - one that succeeds through
integrity, earns trust through transparency, and creates value through ethics.

Thank you for your commitment to these principles.
