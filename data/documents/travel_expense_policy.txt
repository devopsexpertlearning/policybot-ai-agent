TRAVEL AND EXPENSE POLICY
TechCorp Solutions
Effective Date: January 2026
Version 3.0

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes guidelines for business travel and expense reimbursement for all TechCorp Solutions employees, contractors, and authorized representatives conducting company business.

The policy aims to:
- Ensure cost-effective travel arrangements
- Provide clear guidelines for allowable expenses
- Establish timely reimbursement procedures
- Maintain compliance with tax regulations
- Protect employee safety during travel

2. TRAVEL AUTHORIZATION

2.1 Pre-Approval Requirements
All business travel must be pre-approved by:
- Direct manager for domestic travel under $2,000
- Department head for domestic travel over $2,000
- VP or above for international travel
- CFO for travel exceeding $5,000

2.2 Advance Notice
Submit travel requests at least:
- 2 weeks for domestic travel
- 4 weeks for international travel
- 6 weeks for travel requiring visas

2.3 Emergency Travel
In case of emergency, obtain verbal approval from your manager and submit written documentation within 24 hours.

3. AIRFARE

3.1 Booking Requirements
- Use company-approved travel agency or booking platform
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Select most economical fare that meets business needs
- Prefer non-stop flights when price difference is less than $100

3.2 Class of Service
- Domestic flights under 4 hours: Economy class
- Domestic flights over 4 hours: Economy Plus/Premium Economy allowed
- International flights under 6 hours: Economy class
- International flights over 6 hours: Business class allowed for VP and above
- Upgrades using personal funds or miles are permitted

3.3 Baggage Fees
- One checked bag fee is reimbursable
- Additional bags require justification
- Overweight fees not reimbursable unless for company equipment

4. GROUND TRANSPORTATION

4.1 Airport Transportation
Preferred options (in order):
1. Hotel shuttle (free)
2. Shared ride services (Uber/Lyft Pool)
3. Standard ride services (Uber/Lyft)
4. Taxi
5. Rental car (if multiple destinations)
6. Airport parking (if driving personal vehicle)

4.2 Rental Cars
- Compact or mid-size vehicles only
- Full-size allowed for 3+ passengers
- SUVs require special approval
- Decline rental car insurance (covered by corporate policy)
- Refuel before return to avoid premium charges

4.3 Personal Vehicle Use
- Reimbursed at IRS standard mileage rate ($0.655/mile for 2026)
- Must have valid license and insurance
- Calculate mileage using Google Maps or similar
- Not to exceed cost of airfare for trips over 300 miles

4.4 Public Transportation
- Fully reimbursable (trains, subways, buses)
- Keep receipts for amounts over $25

5. LODGING

5.1 Hotel Selection
- Use company-preferred hotel chains when available
- Book through corporate travel platform for negotiated rates
- Select hotels within reasonable proximity to business location
- Maximum rates (per night, excluding taxes):
  * Tier 1 cities (NYC, SF, LA, etc.): $250
  * Tier 2 cities: $175
  * Tier 3 cities: $125
  * International: Varies by location (consult travel team)

5.2 Extended Stays
- Stays over 7 nights: Consider extended-stay hotels or apartments
- Stays over 30 nights: Negotiate monthly rates

5.3 Shared Accommodations
- Employees are not required to share rooms
- Sharing is encouraged for cost savings but voluntary

6. MEALS AND ENTERTAINMENT

6.1 Per Diem Rates
Daily meal allowances (no receipts required under per diem):
- Breakfast: $15
- Lunch: $25
- Dinner: $40
- Total daily: $80

6.2 Actual Expense Method
Alternatively, submit actual meal receipts:
- Must be reasonable and appropriate
- Alcohol limited to $25 per day
- Excessive or lavish meals will not be reimbursed

6.3 Client Entertainment
- Pre-approval required for meals over $100 per person
- Must include business purpose and attendees
- Alcohol allowed in moderation
- Entertainment (shows, golf, etc.) requires VP approval

6.4 Non-Reimbursable Items
- Minibar charges
- In-room movies
- Personal snacks or beverages
- Room service (except when no other options available)

7. OTHER EXPENSES

7.1 Communication
- International phone plan or SIM card: Reimbursable
- Wi-Fi fees: Reimbursable
- Personal calls: Not reimbursable

7.2 Business Supplies
- Printing, shipping, office supplies: Reimbursable with receipt
- Must be business-related

7.3 Laundry and Dry Cleaning
- Reimbursable for trips over 5 consecutive nights
- Maximum $25 per week

7.4 Tips and Gratuities
- Reasonable tips (15-20%) are reimbursable
- Include in expense report with explanation

8. EXPENSE REPORTING

8.1 Submission Timeline
- Submit expense reports within 30 days of trip completion
- Late submissions may be denied
- Outstanding reports block future travel approvals

8.2 Required Documentation
- Original itemized receipts for expenses over $25
- Credit card receipts alone are not sufficient
- Business purpose and attendees for meals
- Conference agendas for event attendance

8.3 Expense Report Platform
- Use Concur expense management system
- Attach digital copies of all receipts
- Categorize expenses correctly
- Provide detailed descriptions

8.4 Reimbursement Timeline
- Approved expenses paid within 10 business days
- Direct deposit to employee bank account
- Check available upon request

9. CORPORATE CREDIT CARDS

9.1 Eligibility
- Available to employees who travel frequently (5+ trips/year)
- Requires manager approval
- Subject to credit check

9.2 Usage Guidelines
- Use only for approved business expenses
- Personal use strictly prohibited
- Report lost/stolen cards immediately
- Reconcile monthly statements in Concur

9.3 Payment Responsibility
- Company pays card balance directly
- Employee responsible for disputed charges
- Failure to reconcile may result in card cancellation

10. INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL

10.1 Additional Requirements
- Valid passport (6+ months validity)
- Necessary visas and work permits
- Travel insurance (provided by company)
- Register trip with corporate security

10.2 Currency Exchange
- Use corporate credit card for foreign transactions
- Avoid currency exchange kiosks (high fees)
- ATM withdrawals reimbursable (limit 2 per trip)

10.3 Vaccinations and Health
- Required vaccinations are reimbursable
- Consult CDC travel recommendations
- Carry prescription medications in original containers

11. TRAVEL SAFETY

11.1 Security Protocols
- Enroll in Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP)
- Share itinerary with manager and emergency contact
- Avoid high-risk areas
- Follow State Department travel advisories

11.2 Emergency Assistance
- 24/7 travel assistance hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX
- Contact local embassy/consulate if needed
- Report incidents to corporate security immediately

12. POLICY VIOLATIONS

12.1 Consequences
- First violation: Written warning
- Second violation: Reimbursement denial
- Third violation: Loss of travel privileges
- Severe violations: Disciplinary action up to termination

12.2 Fraud
- Falsifying expense reports is grounds for immediate termination
- May result in legal action
- Must repay fraudulent amounts

13. EXCEPTIONS AND APPEALS

13.1 Exception Requests
- Submit in writing to Finance department
- Include business justification
- Requires CFO approval

13.2 Appeals Process
- Denied expenses can be appealed within 30 days
- Submit appeal to Finance Director
- Final decision within 15 business days

14. POLICY UPDATES

This policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed. Employees will be notified of changes via email and company intranet.

- HR Department: hr@techcorp.com
